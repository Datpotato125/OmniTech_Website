Ecosystem & Compatibility
The OmniTech ecosystem is a unified platform of personal devices (OmniPhone, OmniBook, OmniWatch, OmniBuds), smart home appliances (OmniFridge, OmniWash, OmniDish, OmniTherm, OmniSecure, OmniLight Hub), and immersive computing devices (OmniVR, OmniGlass) — all powered by a shared operating system called OmniOS, a cross-device intelligence engine called OmniAI, and a cloud platform called OmniCloud. Every product is designed to communicate with every other product automatically. Tasks transfer seamlessly between devices, your AI assistant maintains context across your phone, laptop, and home, and your appliances coordinate intelligently through OmniHome automation routines.

You can incorporate third-party devices into OmniHome routines and automations. Cross-device continuity is the ability to start a task on one OmniTech device and continue it seamlessly on another without any manual steps. For example: you can begin drafting a document on OmniPhone, pick it up mid-sentence on OmniBook, and view it on a virtual display inside OmniVR — with full context, cursor position, and scroll state preserved. This is powered by the OmniOS Continuity Engine and real-time OmniCloud sync, which maintains sub-200ms latency for state synchronization across all your devices.
Products & Specifications
OmniChip is OmniTech's proprietary system-on-chip (SoC) designed specifically for the OmniTech ecosystem. The X1 variant (flagship) includes a dedicated Neural Processing Unit for real-time on-device AI inference, a Hardware Security Module (HSM) for key management and secure boot, and connectivity silicon supporting 5G, Wi-Fi 7, Bluetooth 5.4, and UWB for precise spatial awareness between devices. The M-series is used in OmniBook laptops, optimized for sustained performance, all-day battery life, and AI-accelerated workloads. Because OmniChip is designed in-house, OmniTech can co-optimize hardware and software in ways third-party silicon cannot support.

Privacy & Security
OmniTech is built on a privacy-first architecture. Biometric data (fingerprint, face, voice), health data (heart rate, SpO2, sleep), and personal context processed by OmniAI are handled on-device inside a secure enclave and never uploaded to OmniCloud without your explicit consent. OmniCloud stores only the data you choose to sync, encrypted at rest and in transit. You have granular per-device, per-app, per-feature permission controls, full audit logs of all AI inferences, complete data export in standard formats, and a right-to-delete fulfilled within 30 days with cryptographic verification.
OmniTech operates a zero-trust security model: every device, user, and service authenticates on every interaction with no implicit trust based on network location. OmniChip includes a dedicated Hardware Security Module (HSM) for key management, secure boot, and hardware attestation. Multi-factor biometric authentication (fingerprint, face, and voice) is processed entirely on-device. OmniCloud infrastructure uses encryption at rest and in transit across all data paths. OmniTech runs a public bug bounty program with payouts up to $500,000 for critical vulnerability disclosures.
OmniAI improves through federated learning — a privacy-preserving technique where model updates are computed on your device using your local data, and only aggregated, anonymized gradient updates are shared with OmniTech's cloud infrastructure. Your raw personal data, conversations, health readings, and usage patterns never leave your device as part of the training process. You can opt out of participating in federated learning entirely in OmniCloud privacy settings without affecting any OmniAI functionality.
Enterprise
